The jubilee 50th festival was opened

27.5.2018

Tonight at the Sava Center started the 46th FEST. A festive atmosphere was felt in front of the hall, on a red carpet walked by the guests of the festival and distinguished guests. The opening ceremony leaders, Raša Bukvić and Maria Bergam, reminded the audience of the glorious beginnings of our biggest festival, and then presented films from this year's competition selection. Artistic director, Jugoslav Pantelic, stated that FEST this year offers a cross-section of everything that we can call a quality movie, no matter where it originated. "All 116 films were chosen for a reason, because they were taken by courageous people by treating themes that concern us all. The world has long been a global village, and the best way to understand it is to confront the problems of those who come from some just seeming distant parts of the world. There are no more distant parts of the world, there are distant people that need to be brought closer together. " Pantelic dedicated the festival to all those who are not with us today, who contributed to it in some way. On that occasion, he thanked the founder of FEST, Milutin Colic, who left us on this date nine years ago, as well as the great names that left us in the past year - Ljubisa Samardzic and Nebojsa Glogovac, which the audience greeted with a thrilling applause.

Robert De Niro announced the arrival at the festival

22.5.2018

Famous Hollywood actor Robert de Niro has never hiding his affection for Serbia and the Serbian people, and he is not surprised that he even once said that he believed he had Serbian ancestors. Although he has been involved in the work he has done in our region, few who know that De Niro traveled through our country before his famous life, where he met with a part of Serbian tradition and culture. When he was kindly hosted by his family in the sixties, in his household near Nis, a famous Hollywood actor fell in love with Serbia. We cherish the village of Čokot, a home-made cake and cheese, but also a warm hospitality, won De Niro, who once declared that he "feels like a Serb". In 1963, at the age of 20, he performed in his first film role, in Briana De Palme's The Wedding Party. However, the film was not published until 1969. He spent sixty years working in theatrical workshops and smaller theaters. In the French film The Three Rooms in Manhattan (1965), he performed as a statue, and he made an official debut in De Palmin's movie Greetings (1968), while in 1970 he reprized it in the movie Hello, Mom !.
