Cate Blanchett

Blanchett made an international film debut as an Australian nurse captured by the Japanese in the production of The Road for Raj's director Bruce Beresford, with Glenn Close and Frances McDormand. Her first major role, also in 1997, was Lucinda Leplastrier, produced by Gillian Armstrong Oscar and Lucinda, along with Ralph Fiennes. By the way, Peter Carey, an Australian award-winning writer and author of Oscars and Lucinda, knew Blanchett's father Bob as the two worked in the advertising industry in Melbourne. Blanchett was nominated for her first prize at the Australian Film Institute, but she lost to Pamela Raba. However, in the same year, she won the Best Supporting Actress Award in the romantic comedy Thank God to meet Lizzie, in which she performed with Richard Roxburgh and Frances O'Connor.

That same year he began his fruitful collaboration with Martin Scorsese, performing as a small mobster "Johnny Boy" along with Harvey Keitel in the Streets of Evil (1973). In 1974 De Niro performed as a young Vito Corleone in Kum II director Francis Ford Coppola. The performance brought Oscar for the best supporting actor.
